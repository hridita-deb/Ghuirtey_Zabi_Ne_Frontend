Discover Tourist Spots

Chattogram Spots

Patenga Sea Beach

One of the most popular beaches near the city, located close to the mouth of the Karnaphuli River. It offers stunning sunsets, opportunities for speed boat rides, and views of large ships anchored in the bay.

Bhatiary

A scenic area situated in the Sitakunda Upazila of the Chattogram District, Bangladesh, known for its natural environment featuring hills, lakes, and a prominent golf course.

Cox's Bazar Spots

World's Longest Sea Beach

The main attraction, spanning approximately 120 km. It's famous for its gentle slopes, golden sands, and the opportunity to witness both sunrise and sunset over the Bay of Bengal.

Himchari National Park & Falls

A beautiful area 18 km south of Cox's Bazar, featuring lush green hills, tropical rainforests, and seasonal waterfalls, offering panoramic views of the sea from the hilltops.

Saint Martin Island

Bangladesh's only coral island, offering crystal clear water, abundant marine life, and a quiet escape. A true tropical paradise accessible by ferry from Teknaf.

Bandarban Spots

Nilgiri

A mesmerizing hill resort located at an altitude of approximately 2,200 feet, offering a spectacular view of the clouds and surrounding hills. It's often called the 'Darjeeling of Bangladesh'.

Boga Lake (Dragon Lake)

Boga Lake, perched high in the Bandarban hills, is a natural crater lake surrounded by steep rocky ridges. Its water shifts from deep blue to emerald green depending on the light, held quietly within the bowl-shaped valley. Local folklore ties the lake to ancient stories of the indigenous communities, giving the still water a sense of old mystery. The lake sits over a thousand feet above sea level, isolated from the noise of the plains and bordered by untouched hill forests..

Rangamati Spots

Kaptai Lake

Kaptai Lake, the largest man-made lake in Bangladesh, was formed in the 1960s by damming the Karnaphuli River. Its deep blue water winds through hills and forests, creating long, narrow channels that look like rivers resting between green ridges. Small islands rise from the surface like quiet pieces of land adrift, and the lake's shoreline curves gently around valleys, villages, and old hill paths that now meet the water's edge.

Rangamati Hanging Bridge

The Rangamati Hanging Bridge, known as Jhulonto Bridge, stretches across a quiet arm of Kaptai Lake, its wooden planks connecting two slopes of green hills. Chandpur Spots

Meghna Ghat

The city of Chandpur, Bangladesh, stands on the banks of the Meghna River and is a significant river port, well known for being at the confluence of several major rivers, including the Padma and the Dakatia..

Shahrasti Shahi Mosque

The Hazrat Sayed Shah Rasti (R) Mazar & Mosque Complex is a significant religious and historical site located in the Sreepur village of Shahrasti Upazila, Chandpur District, Bangladesh.

Khagrachari Spots

Alutila Cave (Matai Hakar)

Feni Spots

Old house

The "old house" in Bashpara, Feni, is widely known as the house of Zamindar Binod Bihari, an important historical site in the area. It is also associated with the nearby Sat Math (Seven Temples), which are located on the same eight-acre property in the West Chhagalnaiya village of the Feni district.

Noakhali Spots

Nijhum Dwip

Nijhum Dwip, also known as Nijhum Island, is a serene island in the Bay of Bengal under Hatiya Upazila in Noakhali District of Bangladesh, renowned for its natural beauty, diverse wildlife, and mangrove forests. It is a popular eco-tourism destination, especially known for being a deer sanctuary and a winter home for migratory birds.

Musapur Sea Beach

ocated in the southern part of Companyganj Upazila, Noakhali, Musapur Sea Beach (also known as Musapur Closer) is a tranquil coastal area along the Bay of Bengal. It is known for its serene atmosphere, lush greenery, and stunning sunrises and sunsets. Unlike more crowded beaches, it offers a peaceful environment for relaxation.

Lakshmipur Spots

Dakatia River

The Dakatia River is a 207-kilometre (129 mi) long trans-boundary river that flows through both India (Tripura) and Bangladesh, ultimately joining the Meghna River at Chandpur Boro Station molehead. The riverbanks, particularly around the town of Chandpur, are a significant site of economic activity and environmental concern.

Cumilla Spots

Chandimura Mandir

The Chandi Mata Temple in Cumilla is formally known as Lalmai Chandi Temple or Chandimura Mandir. This ancient Hindu temple complex is situated on the summit of the Lalmai hill in the Barura Upazila of Cumilla District, Bangladesh.

Mainamati

Mainamati, located near Comilla in Bangladesh, is an archaeological site famous for its ancient Buddhist ruins from the 8th to 12th centuries CE. Key sites include the Shalban Vihara monastery, and the area is also home to the Mainamati War Cemetery, which commemorates soldiers from World War II.

Brahmanbaria Spots

Arifail Mosque

The Arifail Mosque (also spelled Arifil) is a historic three-domed mosque located in Sarail Upazila, Brahmanbaria, Bangladesh. It is a protected monument under the Department of Archaeology, Bangladesh.
