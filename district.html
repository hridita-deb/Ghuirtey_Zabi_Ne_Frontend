Chittagong Division Districts

Chattogram

Chattogram (Chittagong) is the commercial capital and main seaport of Bangladesh, historically renowned as the "Queen of the East" and the "Land of the Twelve Saints." Its distinctive geography is a unique convergence of the Bay of Bengal, rolling hills (part of the Chittagong Hill Tracts), and the Karnaphuli River, which hosts the country's main port. The landscape offers diverse natural beauty, from the coastal breeze and sunset views at Patenga Sea Beach to the serene, green hill valleys of Bhatiary, perfect for relaxation and hiking. As a historical trade hub, it also offers a rich cultural heritage, making it a multifaceted destination for both business and leisure travelers.

Cox’s Bazar

Cox's Bazar is globally celebrated as the home of the world's longest natural sea beach, stretching over 120 kilometers. It is the premier coastal tourist destination in Bangladesh, where the endless expanse of sandy beach meets lush green hills. Beyond the main beach, the district is a hub for exploration, including the famous Marine Drive connecting to Himchari's waterfalls and the stunning coral island gateway of Saint Martin's Island. Cox's Bazar seamlessly combines relaxing beach holidays with adventure and cultural excursions.

Bandarban

Bandarban is the heart of the Chittagong Hill Tracts, offering the most rugged and spectacular mountainous landscapes in the country, home to Bangladesh's highest peaks like Tajingdong (Bijoy). It is a paradise for adventurers and nature lovers, famed for its trekking routes, deep forests, and the peaceful Buddha Dhatu Jadi (Golden Temple). The district is rich in cultural diversity, providing a unique opportunity to interact with over a dozen indigenous ethnic groups like the Marma and Murong.

Rangamati

Known as the "Lake City," Rangamati's identity is defined by the expansive, man-made Kaptai Lake, which dominates its geography and serves as the main mode of transportation. The district offers stunning views of water meeting hills, best experienced through a boat ride to spots like the iconic Hanging Bridge and the majestic Shuvolong Waterfall. Rangamati provides a serene escape, combining natural beauty with the vibrant cultural traditions of the indigenous Chakma people.

Khagrachari

Khagrachhari offers a picturesque blend of green rolling hills, valleys, and mysterious natural wonders. It's renowned for the adventurous Alutila Mysterious Cave, a dark, underground cavern that thrills visitors, and scenic natural cascades like Richhang Waterfall. As a key district of the Hill Tracts, it is also a cultural hotspot, allowing travelers to experience the unique lifestyle and festivals of the local Tripura and Marma communities.

Feni

Feni is a border district known for its significant role in the country's history and its impressive water management structures. Its key attraction is the Muhuri Irrigation Project and Sluice Gate, which has transformed the landscape and offers wide, open views often compared to a "mini-sea." The area's flat terrain and numerous historical ponds, like Bijoy Singh Dighi, provide peaceful spots for relaxation and reflection on the region's socio-economic evolution.

Noakhali

Noakhali is a flat, coastal district facing the Bay of Bengal, known for its extensive agricultural lands and newly accreted char (shoal) lands. Its most famous attraction is Nijhum Dwip (Silent Island), a remote and breathtaking national park in the estuary of the Meghna River. This island is a vital sanctuary for spotted deer and migratory birds, offering a unique eco-tourism experience within a vast, serene mangrove forest setting.

Lakshmipur

Lakshmipur is an important riverine district, primarily known for its rich agriculture, especially the production of betel nut and coconut, and its contribution to the country's hilsa fishing industry. Located near the vast Meghna River, the district features various historical zamindar houses (like Dalal Bazar Zamindar Bari) and important Islamic sites, reflecting a deep cultural and economic history rooted in its fertile deltaic land.

Chandpur

Chandpur is famously known as the "Land of Hilsa," serving as a central trading point for the national fish. Its primary natural feature is the dramatic 'Tin Nodir Mohona' (Confluence of Three Rivers), where the mighty Padma, Meghna, and Dakatia rivers merge, creating a powerful and scenic spectacle. As a major river port, the district offers a captivating glimpse into the vibrant river life and trade that sustains the central part of the country.

Brahmanbaria

Brahmanbaria is celebrated as the "Cultural Capital of Bangladesh," boasting a rich heritage, especially in classical Bengali music and literature. This riverine district is interlaced with the Titas River, providing picturesque views and supporting its agricultural base. It features several historically significant sites, including ancient mosques and the Mausoleum of Bir Shreshtha Mostafa Kamal, making it a destination for both cultural enthusiasts and historical exploration.

Cumilla

Cumilla is a historically significant region, famous for its ancient archaeological treasures dating back to the 8th century, particularly the ruins of the Mainamati-Lalmai range and the Buddhist monastic complex of Shalban Vihara. It serves as a vital historical bridge connecting ancient Bengal with the broader subcontinental history. Beyond its ruins, the city is known for its cultural importance, its contribution to the Liberation War (marked by the War Cemetery), and the popular local sweetmeat, Roshmalai.
